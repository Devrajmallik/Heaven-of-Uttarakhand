Ramnagar

It is located approximately 65 kilometres (40 mi) from Nainital, the headquarters of the district. Ramnagar is a gateway to the Corbett National Park, the oldest national park and a famous tourist destination. Jim Corbate National Park

Jim Corbett National Park is a national park in India located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand state. The first national park in India, it was established in 1936 during the British Raj and named Hailey National Park after William Malcolm Hailey, a governor of the United Provinces in which it was then located. In 1956, nearly a decade after India's independence, it was renamed Corbett National Park after the hunter and naturalist Jim Corbett, who had played a leading role in its establishment and had died the year before. The park was the first to come under the Project Tiger initiative. Corbett National Park comprises 520.8 km2 (201.1 sq mi) area of hills, riverine belts, marshy depressions, grasslands and a large lake. The elevation ranges from 1,300 to 4,000 ft (400 to 1,220 m). Winter nights are cold but the days are bright and sunny. It rains from July to September. The park has sub-Himalayan belt geographical and ecological characteristics. Dense moist deciduous forest mainly consists of Shorea robusta (the sal tree), haldu, peepal, rohini and mango trees. Forest covers almost 73 per cent of the park, while 10 per cent of the area consists of grasslands. It houses around 110 tree species, 50 species of mammals, 580 bird species and 25 reptile species. An ecotourism destination, the park contains 617 different species of plants and a diverse variety of fauna. The increase in tourist activities, among other problems, continues to present a serious challenge to the park's ecological balance.

Garjiya Devi Temple

Garjiya Devi Temple is a famous temple located in Uttarakhand . This temple comes in Sundar Khal village. The distance of this temple from Ramnagar is about 15 km. This temple is built on the top of a small hill, which is situated in the middle of Kosi river. Devotees come here to worship and take bath. The bridge leading to the temple is closed during heavy rainy season (July/August). This temple of Goddess Girija, who is the daughter of Giriraj Himalaya and the consort of Lord Shankar, the creator of the world, is situated on a mound in the middle of Kosi (Kaushiki) river. Till the year 1940, very few people knew about this temple, currently by the grace of Girija Mata, the number of compassionate devotees has reached lakhs. This temple was systematically constructed in 1970. In which the present priest of the temple, Pt. Purnachandra Pandey has made an important effort. To get important information about this temple, it is necessary to know its historical and religious background. Mother Parvati, the better half of Lord Shiva, also has a name called Girija, being the daughter of Giriraj Himalaya, she is also called by this name. In this temple, Mother Girija is present in the Satoguni form of Devi. Which becomes happy only with true devotion, here worship is done by offering coir coconut, red clothes, vermillion, incense, lamp etc. When their wishes are fulfilled, devotees offer a bell or an umbrella. Newly married women come here and wish for a happy marriage. Childless couples spread a bag at the feet of their mother in order to have a child.
